Global GDP and Homicide Rates from 1990 to 2010

 

From 1990 to 2010, there is a huge disparity between high-income countries like Canada, Norway and Austria, and low-income ones like the African Ghana, Mali and Cameroon. The GDP per capita for the high-income countries has been increasing year-by-year, with some countries surpassing Int$30,000 by 2010. However, there is little change to the GDP per capita of low-income countries, with many of the aforementioned countries below Int$2,500 by 2010.

 

 

Global homicide rates seem to be decreasing overall, with the majority of the bars by the countries shrinking from 1990 to 2010. However, the peaks of certain bars seem to be increasing. Particularly for countries like El Salvador, Honduras and Lebanon, homicide rates seem to reach sharp crescendos of over 60 per 100,000 people by 2010. Countries like Austria, Finland and Greece have experienced homicide rates of close to 0 per 100,000, and this number has remained steady for these countries.
